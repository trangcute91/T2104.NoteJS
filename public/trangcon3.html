Yeast and things to know

For those of you who have made bread before, it is probably no stranger to yeast, but for those of you who have never done it, you will definitely encounter this type of ingredient when making bread. The reason is because yeast is an indispensable catalyst in the bread making process so that the bread can expand, spongy and create the characteristic flavor of bread. To better understand how yeast works and how to use it in the baking process, let's find out in this article!

First, what is yeast and how does it work?

In a scientific and complete way, yeast is a microorganism capable of secreting a number of enzymes that help promote fermentation in bread dough, help metabolize proteins in bread to produce alcohol and gas. -bon. The carbon gas will be captured by the gluten fibers, expanding to create a spongy bread, while the alcohol will evaporate during the baking process.

In a nutshell, yeast is a substance that converts ingredients in bread dough to produce carbon dioxide (through the fermentation process) to make bread (a process similar to making alcohol).

Unlike baking soda or baking powder (baking agents in cakes), because yeast is a living microorganism, yeast only works in a suitable temperature environment, between 27-35 degrees Celsius. If the environment is high or lower than this, the yeast will be weak or even inactive, affecting the baking process (the cake does not bloom).

Baking yeast on the market has many different types and brands, but in terms of properties, it will be divided into three main groups:

-Fresh yeast/ Compressed yeast: Fresh yeast is usually made into cakes, slightly moist and easy to crumble (see more pictures for an easy idea), often used by professional chefs because of its delicious taste. when making bread. Fresh yeast is mixed directly into the dough or mixed first with warm water for about 5 minutes for the yeast to work (the mixture is smooth, slightly foamy) and then mixed with the flour to ensure the yeast works well. The drawback of fresh yeast is that it is short in use, often packed into large cakes, difficult to preserve for a long time.

-Dry yeast: This type is divided into 2 groups: "Active dry yeast" and "Instant dry yeast". You can see the difference between the two with the naked eye

However, to make sure to use yeast properly (even the easiest one is instant yeast), there are a few points to keep in mind:

– Suitable ambient temperature for yeast to work well: 27-35 degrees. In a word, the ingredients used in the recipe must be left at room temperature when making the cake.

-In the case of using yeast that needs to be activated, the temperature of the liquid used to activate the yeast must not be too hot (killing the yeast) or too cold (inhibiting the yeast). A thermometer can be used to ensure the required temperature.

-Salt is a spice that has the ability to inhibit yeast activity, so when mixing ingredients, limit contact with yeast and salt (do not add salt at the same time or mix salt with other ingredients before adding salt). enter).
