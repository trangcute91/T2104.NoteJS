Butter and fats in baking

In baking, Butter along with Flour, Sugar, Eggs, Milk are the basic ingredients and always appear in every cake recipe from basic to complex. Butter plays a very important role not only in baking but also in Western cuisine in general, so this analysis is written to honor… oh wrong, so that you can better understand its characteristics, classification and usage. Use and store butter properly. In this article, Thuan will also mention some other types of fat that you will occasionally come across in cake recipes!

Butter

Of the fats used in baking, Butter is the most commonly mentioned and is always present in most European cake recipes. Based on its origin, Butter is divided into 2 groups: Butter and Margarine.

1.Animal butter.

Butter, referred to as butter, is a dairy product obtained after the process of whipping cow's milk or fermenting fresh cream. Butter is made up of 85% milk fat and 15% water, in which the solid fat crystals in milk fat are the agents that help the butter to coagulate when it is cold instead of melting like other butters. other animal fats (fat, etc.).

\

Two types of butter that are commonly sold in the market are unsalted butter and salted butter. Unsalted butter is used a lot in cooking and baking to create fat, richness and aroma of dishes while salted butter is often used directly with bread, cheese or nuts and dried fruits.

2.Margarine

Also known as Margarine, Margarine is made from vegetable oils (soybean/sunflower/…) that undergo hydrogenation to form a hard, flexible form. Because of its plant origin, Margarine is often used as a substitute for butter or for vegetarians.

In baking, some recipes can replace margarine with margarine, but the taste will not be as fragrant and fatty as margarine.
